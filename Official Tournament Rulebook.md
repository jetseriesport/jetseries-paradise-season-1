# JetSeries Paradise Season 1 - Official Tournament Rulebook

## Event overview
JetSeries Paradise Season 1 is an open **Counter-Strike 2 LAN tournament** organized by JetSeries.  
The tournament is designed as a competitive offline event and is intended to be eligible for **Valve Regional Standings (VRS)**, subject to confirmation by HLTV.org and Valve.  

JetSeries Paradise Season 1 follows Valve’s official rules for ranked tournaments to ensure competitive integrity and a fair tournament structure.  
This rulebook outlines all regulations, format details, and expectations for teams participating in JetSeries Paradise Season 1.

## Event Information
- **Event Name:** JetSeries Paradise Season 1 — Counter-Strike 2 Open LAN  
- **Time Zone:** All times listed follow Batumi local time (BAT)  
- **Tier & Sanctioning:** Tier 2 VRS-ranked event (results contribute to Valve’s regional standings)  
- **Dates:** May 22 – May 25, 2026  
- **Location:** Mercury, Batumi, Georgia

### Team Cap
- Minimum of 4 and maximum of 16 teams may participate.  
- Registration is **first-come, first-served**.  
- Each team consists of **5 players**, with the option to register **1 coach**.  
- All participants must attend **on-site in Batumi**.

- ### Registration
- Teams must register via the official JetSeries registration form.  
- **Registration fee:** $2,000 USD per team to confirm entry  
- Sign-ups close automatically on **May 15, 2026 at 23:59 (BAT)**.

- ### Prize Pool
The prize pool is fully crowdfunded by team entry fees and scales with the number of registered teams:

| Teams | Prize Pool |
|-------|------------|
| 4     | $2,500     |
| 8     | $5,000     |
| 16    | $10,000    |

## Registration & Eligibility

### Team Eligibility
- Open to all teams in the **Global region**, as defined by Valve’s rules.  
- No prior qualification or minimum rank required.  
- All players must have valid CS2 accounts in good standing (**no VAC bans**).  
- Players must be **at least 16 years old** (under 18 may require parental consent).

### Sign-up Process
- Teams register via the official JetSeries registration form.  
- Payment of **$2,000 USD per team** secures the spot.  
- Team representative will receive additional instructions from JetSeries to confirm participation.

### Roster Rules
- Teams may register up to **5 players + 1 coach**.  
- Roster is locked **14 days before the event**.  
- After this date, teams must retain **at least 3 out of 5 core players**.

### VRS Seeding
- All 16 teams start in the **Group Stage**.  
- No team is directly seeded into the Playoffs.  
- After the Group Stage, the top 8 teams advance to **Double-Elimination Playoffs**.  
- VRS rankings may be referenced for Group Stage seeding, but progression to the Main Event is **performance-based**.

### Check-in
- Teams must check in at the venue on **Day 1** by the designated time.  
- Schedule available on the official [**JetSeries GitHub page**](https://github.com/jetseriesport/jetseries-paradise-season-1) and [**Telegram page**](https://t.me/jetleague).
- Teams must be on-site **1 hour before their first match**.  
- Failure to check in may result in disqualification.

- ### On-site Conduct
- Teams must abide by venue rules and tournament policies.  
- Respect towards staff, opponents, and facility is mandatory.  
- Withdrawals within 14 days or no-shows result in **forfeiture** of registration fees and scheduled matches.

- ## Tournament Format

### Overview of Stages
JetSeries Paradise Season 1 spans **4 days**:

- **Day 1–2 – Group Stage:** 16 teams compete in 4 GSL groups of 4. Winners and runners-up advance to Playoffs.  
- **Day 3–4 – Playoffs:** 8-team double-elimination bracket from Group Stage qualifiers.

### Group Stage (Days 1–2)
- **Participants:** All 16 teams  
- **Group Formation:** 4 groups of 4 teams each  

**Seeding:**
- **Ranked teams:** Assigned to groups according to VRS ranking.  
- **Unranked teams:** Randomly assigned among unranked pool.  
- Hybrid seeding ensures balanced groups.

**Group Format (GSL):**
- **Opening Matches:** Best-of-3  
- **Winners Match:** Best-of-3  
- **Elimination Match:** Best-of-3  
- **Decider Match:** Best-of-3

- **Match Rules:**
- Overtime enabled; draws not possible.  
- Best-of-1 ending 12–12 → MR3 OT, $10,000 start money.  
- Best-of-3 follows same OT rules.

- **Advancement:** Group winner + runner-up advance to Playoffs (total 8 teams).
- 
**Scheduling:** Matches in waves over 2 days.  
- Teams ready **15 min before match**.  
- Short breaks (5–10 min) between matches.  
- Group Stage concludes by evening of Day 2.

- ### Playoffs (Days 3–4)
- **Participants:** 8 teams from Group Stage  
- **Bracket Format:** Double-Elimination  
- **Match Format:** Best-of-3 (Grand Final included)  

**Seeding:** Group winners vs. runners-up from different groups  

| Match # | Group Stage Position 1 | Group Stage Position 2 |
|---------|-----------------------|-----------------------|
| 1       | 1st Group A Winner    | 2nd Group B Runner-up |
| 2       | 1st Group B Winner    | 2nd Group A Runner-up |
| 3       | 1st Group C Winner    | 2nd Group D Runner-up |
| 4       | 1st Group D Winner    | 2nd Group C Runner-up |

**Grand Final:**  
- Upper Bracket winner vs Lower Bracket winner, Best-of-3  
- No bracket reset, no map advantage  
- Winner-take-all

### Match Rules
- Standard CS2 competitive settings  
- Best-of-3 → MR12, OT if needed  
- Forfeits handled per **Conduct & Admin Policies**

- ### Match Scheduling and Punctuality
- Detailed schedule provided to teams  
- Teams **ready 15 min before match**, all equipment set up  
- Short grace period (up to 10 min) allowed  
- Downtime minimized:  
  - 10 min breaks between maps in Bo3  
  - 30 min between consecutive matches  
- Schedule adjustments require admin permission

- ## General Game Settings

- **Game Version:** Counter-Strike 2 (latest updated version on Steam by January 30)  
- **Platform:** LAN servers provided by the tournament, configured with standard competitive settings. No third-party anti-cheat required, but admins actively monitor for suspicious activity.  
- **Account:** Players must use their own Steam/CS2 accounts.  
  - Account sharing is **not permitted**  
  - Must own CS2 and have **no VAC or competitive bans**  
- All matches use official CS2 competitive configuration.  

### Match Parameters
- **Match Format:** MR24 – first team to 13 rounds wins  
- **Round Time:** 1 minute 55 seconds  
- **Bomb Timer:** 40 seconds  
- **Freeze Time:** 20 seconds  
- **Buy Time:** 20 seconds  
- **Start Money:** $800 on pistol round  

### Overtime Rules
- Trigger: Score 12–12 after regulation  
- **Format:** MR3 – 3 rounds per side  
- **Start Money:** $10,000 per team  
- Teams swap sides after 3 OT rounds  
- Repeat OT periods until a winner is determined  

### Side & Map Selection

#### Side Selection
- **First Map of the Match:** Higher-seeded team chooses either:  
  1. Map veto order  
  2. Starting side  

- **Subsequent Maps in Bo3:**  
  - Side selection follows the veto process  
  - Team picking the map: opponent chooses starting side  

- **Overtime:**  
  - Teams continue on the side they ended regulation  
  - Swap sides after 3 OT rounds  
  - OT continues in increments of 3 rounds per side until a winner  

#### Map Pool
- **Official Active Duty map pool** at event time  
- 7 maps:  
  1. Inferno  
  2. Mirage  
  3. Nuke  
  4. Ancient  
  5. Overpass  
  6. Dust2  
  7. Anubis  

### Veto Process
- All matches use veto/pick system for map and side selection  
- Follows **Valve competitive standards**  
- Overseen by tournament admin  
- Conducted on-stage, via server, or online veto tool  
- Teams have **1–2 minutes per pick/ban**  

#### Seeding Determination
- **Seeded Teams:** Placed according to VRS rankings  
- **Unranked Teams:** Assigned randomly  
- Group Stage seeding ensures balanced groups  
- Playoffs: no VRS-based seeding; matchups are **group winners vs runners-up**  

**Higher-seeded team priority:**  
- In Group Stage: better pre-tournament VRS ranking or random if unranked  
- In Playoffs: better Group Stage placement  
- Chooses either:  
  1. Map veto order  
  2. Starting side  

### Pick/Ban Process

#### Best-of-1 (Bo1)
1. Higher seed chooses Team A or B  
2. Team A removes 2 maps  
3. Team B removes 3 maps  
4. Team A removes 1 map  
5. Remaining map is played  
6. Team B chooses starting side  

#### Best-of-3 (Bo3)
1. Higher seed chooses Team A or B  
2. Team A removes 1 map  
3. Team B removes 1 map  
4. Team A picks Map 1 → Team B chooses starting side  
5. Team B picks Map 2 → Team A chooses starting side  
6. Team B removes 1 map  
7. Team A removes 1 map  
8. Remaining map = Map 3 (decider) → Team B chooses starting side  

### Match Server and Config
- Hosted on tournament servers with standard config  
- Teams may load personal exec/configs before match start  
- Match starts once both teams signal readiness  
- Failure to report config issues before first round = match proceeds as-is  

### Pauses and Timeout Rules

#### Tactical Pauses (Timeouts)
- Each team: up to **3 timeouts per map** + 1 in Overtime  
- Duration: **30 seconds**  
- Initiated via in-game menu command  
- Multiple timeouts in a row allowed if needed  

#### Technical Pauses
- Trigger: hardware malfunction, disconnect, server lag  
- Type `!tech` in chat to request pause  
- Only legitimate issues allowed  
- During pause: players/coaches remain at positions; no discussion allowed

- #### Admin Pauses
- Admins may pause at any time to resolve issues  
- Players/coaches remain at positions; no discussion allowed  

#### Duration of Pauses
- Tactical: 30 seconds per timeout  
- Technical: as needed, max ~10 minutes  
- If problem persists, admins may continue match or enforce forfeit  
- Teams should have backup equipment ready

## Match Interruptions & Recovery

- **Server Crash / All Players Drop:**  
  - Round in progress is nullified and replayed  
  - Admins use CS2 match recovery tools if available  
  - Managers, coaches, or staff **cannot intervene**  
  - Violations may result in forfeits or disqualification  

- **Player Disconnects:**  
  - Single player disconnect: team may request technical pause  
  - If early in round (no significant action), admin may restart round  
  - If significant action occurred, round continues with remaining players  
  - Multiple disconnects or major mid-round issues: admins may replay round  

- **Match Medics:**  
  - Admins may use CS2 match restore to recover game state  
  - If unavailable, round score is restored and next round starts normally  
  - Extreme cases: map restart may occur with admin approval  

- **No Replay for Misbuys/Team Errors:**  
  - Team mistakes (wrong buy, alt-tab disconnects) are **not replayed**  

### Map Bugs and Glitches

- **Forbidden Exploits:**  
  - Clipping through walls/floors/ceilings  
  - Pixel walking or unintended vantage points  
  - Boosts that cause clipping  
  - Scripts/macros for unfair movement  

- **Allowed Maneuvers:**  
  - Standing on teammates (if not glitching)  
  - Surfing allowed if no glitch  
  - Wallbangs and defusing through allowed areas legal  

- **Newly Discovered Bugs:**  
  - Admins issue rulings on unlisted bugs  
  - Teams should ask admins if unsure

- **In-Game Communication:** Only necessary communications; abuse punished  
- **Player Behavior:** Sportsmanship required; harassment or toxic behavior penalized

- **Technical Forfeits:** Teams unable/unwilling to continue forfeit maps/matches

- **Integrity of Results:** Faking results or match-fixing prohibited  
- **Betting:** Betting on matches prohibited
